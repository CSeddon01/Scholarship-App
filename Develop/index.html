2022 Patriot Scholarship Award Requirements

The Society of Martha Washington awards patriot scholarships to deserving students to attend close up Washington DC Flagship Program. This civic education program is 6 days/ 5 nights and features meetings with elected officials on Capitol Hill, structured learning activities at Washington’s stunning monuments and memorials and the chance to live and learn with students from other schools nationwide. Covid pandemic protocols are in place for the safety of all participants.

Eligibility Requirements

  • High school juniors or seniors male or female
  • Grade point average of at least 85 (4.5 /6.0)
  • Extra-curricular activities involvement
  • United States Citizen or Legal Resident

Application Requirements

  • Society of Martha Washington application
  • Recent photo
  • Letters of recommendation from teachers and or counselors
  • High School Transcript
  • Photocopy of the first page of previous years 1040 tax form filed by each parent in the household
  • One essay, minimum 350,maximum 500 on either topic
    • Describe your understanding achievement in depth and provide the specific planning, training, goals, and steps taken to make the accomplishment successful. Include details about your role in highlight leadership you provided
    • Why do you like to go to Washington DC for the close-up program? What can you share with students from other places? What do you want to learn from others? What would you like to know about our government? What does “Patriotism” mean to you
  • Covid Pandemic Safety Requirements:
    • Mask worn at all indoor events
    • Full Covid immunization
